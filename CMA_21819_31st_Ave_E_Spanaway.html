Key competition to monitor: 21701 31st Ave E (same street, no shop, currently $750K, 61 days on market with no offers and two price cuts). Condition caveat: Figures assume average/original 2003 interior condition. A 22-year-old home with original kitchen and baths will be perceived as dated by many buyers — this is already baked into the tier pricing. If the walkthrough reveals meaningful updates (flooring, counters, appliances), the aspirational tier gains credibility. If the home shows hard wear, trim each tier by an additional $10K–$20K.

Roof note: An original 2003 composition roof (~22 years) will come up in inspection and may trigger a lender flag on FHA/VA loans (typically flagged at 20–25 years). Consider either (a) replacing pre-listing to remove the objection entirely, or (b) pricing with a buyer credit built in. A new roof is a strong marketing headline on a 5-acre rural property.

Septic note: Recommend pumping before listing. The 11/2020 pump report is professional documentation that buyers and their agents will appreciate — but at 5.5 years out, a fresh pump + new report eliminates a common buyer concern and costs very little relative to deal certainty.

Gas fireplace / heating: If the permitted gas fireplace also brought a gas service line that supplies the water heater or any supplemental heat, that meaningfully reduces the baseboard-only stigma. Confirm and market it explicitly if so.
